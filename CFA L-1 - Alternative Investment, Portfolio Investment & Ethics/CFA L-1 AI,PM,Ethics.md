## Alternative Investments

Alternative investments expand beyond traditional asset classes and provide diversification, illiquidity premiums, and access to unique return drivers.

---

### Characteristics of Alternative Investments

- Low correlation with traditional assets  
- Illiquidity and longer investment horizons  
- Higher complexity and specialized knowledge requirements  
- Active management-driven returns  

---

### Investment Structures

Fund investing involves pooled capital managed by professionals:

$$
\text{Total Fees} = mV_1 + \max[0, p(V_1 - V_0)]
$$

After-fee return:

$$
R_{after} = \frac{V_1 - V_0 - \text{Fees}}{V_0}
$$

Where:  
- \( m \) = Management fee  
- \( p \) = Performance fee  

---

### Performance Measurement

Money-weighted return (IRR) is commonly used due to timing of cash flows.

Money multiple:

$$
\text{Multiple} = \frac{\text{Total Value Received}}{\text{Total Capital Invested}}
$$

---

### Leverage in Returns

$$
R_L = \frac{r(V_0 + V_B) - r_B V_B}{V_0}
$$

Where:  
- \( V_B \) = Borrowed capital  
- \( r_B \) = Borrowing rate  

---

### Private Equity & Debt

- Higher expected returns with higher risk  
- Illiquidity premium  
- Exit strategies: IPO, trade sale, recapitalization  

---

### Real Assets

Includes real estate and infrastructure:

- Stable cash flows  
- Inflation hedge  
- Long-term investment horizon  

---

### Commodities Pricing

$$
F \approx S(1 + r) + \text{Storage Cost} - \text{Convenience Yield}
$$

---

### Hedge Funds

Return decomposition:

- Market beta  
- Strategy beta  
- Alpha  

---

### Digital Assets

- Based on blockchain / distributed ledger technology  
- High volatility and speculative pricing  
- Low historical correlation with traditional assets

## Portfolio Management

Portfolio management focuses on optimizing risk and return through diversification, asset allocation, and systematic investment frameworks.

---

### Risk and Return

Expected return:

$$
E(R_p) = \sum w_i E(R_i)
$$

Portfolio variance:

$$
\sigma_p^2 = w_1^2\sigma_1^2 + w_2^2\sigma_2^2 + 2w_1w_2\rho_{12}\sigma_1\sigma_2
$$

---

### Diversification

- Reduces unsystematic risk  
- Depends on correlation between assets  
- Maximum benefit when correlation is low or negative  

---

### Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

$$
E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i (E(R_m) - R_f)
$$

Where:  
- \( R_f \) = Risk-free rate  
- \( \beta \) = Systematic risk  

---

### Beta Calculation

$$
\beta_i = \frac{\text{Cov}(R_i, R_m)}{\sigma_m^2}
$$

---

### Portfolio Optimization

Efficient frontier represents optimal risk-return combinations.

Capital Market Line (CML):

$$
E(R_p) = R_f + \frac{E(R_m) - R_f}{\sigma_m} \cdot \sigma_p
$$

---

### Performance Metrics

Sharpe Ratio:

$$
\text{Sharpe} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}
$$

Jensen’s Alpha:

$$
\alpha = R_p - [R_f + \beta_p(R_m - R_f)]
$$

---

### Risk Management

Value at Risk (VaR):

$$
\text{VaR}_{\alpha} = \text{Minimum loss at confidence level } \alpha
$$

## Ethics

Ethics is fundamental to the investment profession, ensuring trust, transparency, and integrity.

---

### Core Principles

- Integrity and professionalism  
- Client-first approach  
- Independence and objectivity  
- Fair dealing and disclosure  

---

### Ethical Decision Framework

1. Identify stakeholders and conflicts  
2. Evaluate alternatives  
3. Act with integrity  
4. Reflect on outcomes  

---

### Code of Ethics

Members must:

- Act with integrity, competence, and diligence  
- Place client interests above personal interests  
- Maintain independence and objectivity  
- Promote integrity of capital markets  

---

### Standards of Professional Conduct

1. Professionalism  
2. Integrity of Capital Markets  
3. Duties to Clients  
4. Duties to Employers  
5. Investment Analysis  
6. Conflicts of Interest  
7. Responsibilities as CFA Candidate  

---

### Common Ethical Risks

- Conflicts of interest  
- Misrepresentation  
- Insider trading  
- Market manipulation
